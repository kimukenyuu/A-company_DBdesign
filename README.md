# Blending Tea Startup Database System Design & Implementation

An end-to-end database design project completed as part of the core course **Database Design and Implementation**. The purpose of this project was to interview a real company that required a database system, analyze its business requirements, design an appropriate database schema, and derive SQL queries that could support actual business operations.

- **Team Size:** 4 members  
- **My Role (Team Leader):** Overall project management, interviews with company stakeholders, ERD design, and SQL development

## 1. Project Description

This project was carried out using the concepts learned in the major course **Database Design and Implementation**. The final goal of the project was to interview a real company in need of a database system, design a database based on its requirements, and derive several SQL queries using that database.

## 2. Target Company

**Company A** (anonymized at the request of company officials) is a small startup that sells blending tea. Blending tea refers to a new tea product created by combining an existing tea base (e.g., green tea or chamomile) with other teas or ingredients to create a new flavor profile.

A distinctive feature of Company A is that its blending teas are made by Arab residents living in South Korea. Company A acquires the raw materials used in blending tea from suppliers, has Arab blenders create the teas using those materials, and sells the products while providing blenders with incentives or royalties.

Below are the business rules and requirements that Company A requested our database design team to reflect in the database system.

---

## 3. User Requirements & Business Rules

### 3.1 Customer Management

1. Customers are classified into **Non-members**, **Regular Members**, and **Subscribers**.
   - A **Non-member** is a customer who belongs to neither the Regular Member nor Subscriber category.
   - A customer may simultaneously be both a **Regular Member** and a **Subscriber**.

2. Every customer stores the following common information:
   - `Customer_ID` (Unique Identifier)
   - `Name`
   - `Address` (`Si`, `Dong`, `Ho`)
   - `PhoneNumber` (multiple phone numbers may be recorded)
   - `Gender`
   - `BirthDate`
   - `LoginID`
   - `LoginPassword`

   **Note:** In the case of a non-member, `LoginID` and `LoginPassword` may be `NULL` or replaced with a value such as the customer’s phone number.

3. A **Regular Member** accumulates loyalty points (`Point`) whenever purchase-related activities occur.
   - Non-members do not receive such benefits.
   - Subscribers do not earn points for subscription activity because they pay regularly through a fixed-rate plan rather than through irregular purchase amounts.
   - However, even if a customer is a subscriber, points may still be accumulated when making a separate individual purchase as a regular customer.

4. A **Subscriber** must store a `CreditCardNumber` because recurring payment is required.
   - Each subscriber chooses one subscription plan (`Rate_Plan`).
   - Each subscription plan may be subscribed to by multiple subscribers.

5. `Rate_Plan` stores:
   - `Plan_ID` (Unique Identifier)
   - `PlanName`
   - `Price`
   - `ProvidedCount` (the number of teas provided monthly)

   Only customers who actually subscribe are stored in the subscriber database, whereas subscription plans must remain in the database even if no customer is currently subscribed to them, since they may be chosen by future customers.

---

### 3.2 Order Management

6. Company A wants to store order information generated by customers.
   - `Order` includes:
     - `Order_ID` (Unique Identifier)
     - `OrderDate`
   - Each customer may place multiple orders.
   - Each order is placed by exactly one customer.
   - Only orders generated by customers may be stored in the database.
   - Customers who have never placed an order may still exist in the database.

---

### 3.3 Blending Tea Management

7. Company A wants to store the following information for each `BlendingTea`:
   - `Tea_ID`
   - `Name`
   - `Stock`
   - `Price`

   Each blending tea may be ordered in multiple orders, and each order may contain multiple blending teas. In other words, the relationship between `Order` and `BlendingTea` is many-to-many.

   A `BlendingTea` that has never been ordered may still exist in the database, whereas only orders in which a blending tea has actually been ordered are stored. When a blending tea is ordered, the ordered `Quantity` must also be recorded.

---

### 3.4 Material Management

8. Blending tea is produced by combining existing tea with other teas or ingredients. Company A wants to store these ingredients in `Material`.
   - `Material` includes:
     - `Material_ID` (Unique Identifier)
     - `Name`
     - `Stock`
     - `Price`

   Each blending tea may be produced using multiple materials, and each material may be used in the production of multiple blending teas. Since blending tea is produced from materials, every blending tea must be associated with at least one material. However, a material that has not yet been used in the production of any blending tea may still exist in the database for future use.

   In addition, when a material is used to produce a blending tea, the amount used (`Amount`) must be recorded.

   **Note:** Company A signs contracts with suppliers who provide materials according to the target price desired by Company A, rather than storing the supplier’s quoted price. This is why our team placed the `Price` attribute on the `Material` relation type rather than on the supplier side during ERD design.

---

### 3.5 Supplier Management

9. Each material is supplied by a `Supplier`.
   - `Supplier` stores:
     - `Supplier_ID` (Unique Identifier)
     - `Name`
     - `Address` (`Si`, `Dong`, `Ho`)
     - `PhoneNumber`

   Each supplier may supply multiple materials, and each material may also be supplied by multiple suppliers.

   A material that has never been supplied may still exist in the database. However, only suppliers who have supplied at least one material are stored in the database.

   Whenever a supply event occurs, the supply date and supplied quantity must be recorded.

   **Note:** In the relationship between `Supplier` and `Material`, the `Date` attribute was planned to be used as part of the primary key when converting the ERD into the relational model. It therefore forms a composite primary key in the format `Material_ID + Supplier_ID + Date` so that each supply event can be tracked individually.

---

### 3.6 Blender and Employee Management

10. `BlendingTea` is created by a `Blender`.
   - `Blender` stores:
     - `Blender_ID` (Unique Identifier)
     - `Name`
     - `Age`
     - `Address` (`Si`, `Dong`, `Ho`)
     - `PhoneNumber`
     - `Gender`

   Some blenders may sign a contract with Company A before they have created any blending tea. Therefore, a blender who has not yet made any blending tea may still exist in the database. However, every blending tea must have an associated blender.

   Company A pays a certain amount of `Royalty` to the blender whenever the blending tea is sold.

11. Each blender is assigned exactly one `Employee` who manages them.
   - `Employee` stores:
     - `Employee_ID` (Unique Identifier)
     - `Name`
     - `Address` (`Si`, `Dong`, `Ho`)
     - `PhoneNumber`
     - `Salary`

   An employee may manage multiple blenders.

12. Company A also operates a one-to-one internal training structure between senior and junior employees.
   - In many cases, such mentoring relationships are likely to occur between lower-level staff such as junior employees and assistant managers.
   - On the other hand, experienced employees without a mentor/mentee relationship, or employees in departments where such internal training is not necessary, may not participate in this training system.
   - The company provides differentiated `Incentive` payments according to the training period, and therefore records:
     - `StartDate`
     - `EndDate`
     - `Incentive`

---

### 3.7 Department Management

13. Company A has multiple departments and wants to store them in `Department`.
   - `Department` stores:
     - `Department_ID` (Unique Identifier)
     - `Name`
     - `PhoneNumber`
     - `FAXNumber`
     - `Location`

   Each department may include from one to many employees, and every employee must belong to exactly one department.

---

### 3.8 Lecture Management

14. To improve understanding of Arab culture, Company A requires employees to take at least one `Lecture`.
   - `Lecture` stores:
     - `Lecture_ID` (Unique Identifier)
     - `Name`
     - `TutorName`
     - `Time`
     - `Place`

   Each employee may take multiple lectures, and each lecture may be attended by multiple employees. A lecture that has not yet been taken by any employee may still exist in the database.

   When an employee takes a lecture, the system must record:
   - the date on which the lecture was taken,
   - and the grade received in the quiz conducted after the lecture.

## 5. Logical Design (Relational Schema)

### Schema Definition

- `Department(Department_ID, DepartmentName, DepartmentPhoneNum, FAXNumber, Location)`

- `Employee(Employee_ID, EmployeeName, Si, Dong, Ho, PhoneNumber, Salary, Department_ID, Mentor_ID, StartDate, EndDate, Incentive)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Department_ID) REFERENCES Department(Department_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Mentor_ID) REFERENCES Employee(Employee_ID)`

- `Lecture(Lecture_ID, LectureName, TutorName, Time, Place)`

- `Lecture_Taken(Employee_ID, Lecture_ID, Date, Grade)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Employee_ID) REFERENCES Employee(Employee_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Lecture_ID) REFERENCES Lecture(Lecture_ID)`

- `Blender(Blender_ID, Name, Age, Si, Dong, Ho, PhoneNumber, Gender, Employee_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Employee_ID) REFERENCES Employee(Employee_ID)`

- `BlendingTea(Tea_ID, Name, Stock, Price, Blender_ID, Royalty)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Blender_ID) REFERENCES Blender(Blender_ID)`

- `Material(Material_ID, Name, Stock, Price)`

- `BlendingTea_Manufactured(Tea_ID, Material_ID, Amount)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Tea_ID) REFERENCES BlendingTea(Tea_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Material_ID) REFERENCES Material(Material_ID)`

- `Supplier(Supplier_ID, Name, Si, Dong, Ho, PhoneNumber)`

- `Material_Supplied(Material_ID, Supplier_ID, Quantity, Date)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Material_ID) REFERENCES Material(Material_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Supplier_ID) REFERENCES Supplier(Supplier_ID)`

- `Customer(Customer_ID, Name, Si, Dong, Ho, LoginID, LoginPassword, Gender, BirthDate)`

- `Customer_PhoneNumber(Customer_ID, PhoneNumber)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Customer_ID) REFERENCES Customer(Customer_ID)`

- `Regular(Customer_ID, Point)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Customer_ID) REFERENCES Customer(Customer_ID)`

- `Rate_Plan(Plan_ID, PlanName, Price, ProvidedCount)`

- `Subscriber(Customer_ID, CreditCardNumber, Plan_ID, SubscribeDate)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Customer_ID) REFERENCES Customer(Customer_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Plan_ID) REFERENCES Rate_Plan(Plan_ID)`

- `Order(Order_ID, OrderDate, Customer_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Customer_ID) REFERENCES Customer(Customer_ID)`

- `BlendingTea_Ordered(Tea_ID, Order_ID, Quantity)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Tea_ID) REFERENCES BlendingTea(Tea_ID)`
  - `FOREIGN KEY (Order_ID) REFERENCES Order(Order_ID)`

### Normalization Check

- **1NF:** Satisfied, because every attribute in each relation is atomic.
- **2NF:** Satisfied, because every non-primary-key attribute is fully functionally dependent on the primary key, meaning there is no partial functional dependency.
- **3NF:** Satisfied, because there is no transitive functional dependency.

## 8. Useful SQL Queries

### 1. Updating Blending Tea Stock When Order `O0012` Is Placed

```sql
UPDATE 
(
SELECT stock,quantity
FROM blendingtea, blendingtea_ordered, ordertable
WHERE blendingtea.tea_id = blendingtea_ordered.tea_id 
AND blendingtea_ordered.order_id = ordertable.order_id
AND ordertable.order_id = 'O0012'
)
SET stock = stock - quantity;
````

**Explanation:**  
Because the stock of the blending tea must be reduced by the ordered quantity for order `O0012`, it is necessary to determine through a join which blending tea (`tea_id`) was included in which order (`order_id`). By joining the tables as above and subtracting the ordered `quantity` in `blendingtea_ordered` from the `stock` in `blendingtea`, the stock is updated accordingly.

After executing this SQL statement, the stock of `T0014`, which had been 200, is reduced by the ordered quantity of 88 for order `O0012`, resulting in a stock of 112.

***

### 2. Updating Material Stock When Supplier `S0012` Supplies Material `M0005`

```sql
UPDATE 
(
SELECT stock, quantity
FROM material, material_supplied, supplier
WHERE material.material_id = material_supplied.material_id
AND material_supplied.supplier_id = supplier.supplier_id
AND supplier.supplier_id = 'S0012'
AND material.material_id = 'M0005'
)
SET stock = stock+quantity;
```

**Explanation:**  
When supplies are received, stock should increase accordingly. In this case, the query assumes that supplier `S0012` provides raw material `M0005`. To update inventory, it is first necessary to trace through a join which supplier (`supplier_id`) supplied which material (`material_id`). By following that relationship through `material_supplied` and adding its `quantity` to the `stock` of `material`, the inventory update is completed.

***

### 3. Identifying the Best-Selling Blending Tea

```sql
SELECT tea_id, tea_name, sell 
FROM (SELECT tea_id, SUM(quantity) as sell from blendingtea_ordered GROUP BY tea_id) NATURAL JOIN blendingtea 
WHERE sell = (SELECT MAX(sell) FROM (SELECT tea_id,SUM(quantity) as sell from blendingtea_ordered GROUP BY tea_id));
```

**Explanation:**  
This query is used to identify the best-selling blending tea and its total sales volume. It first calculates the sum of sold quantities for each tea from the current order records, and then retrieves the ID, name, and sales volume of the tea with the highest total sales.

***

### 4. View Showing the Number of Teas Made by Each Blender and Their Total Sales Volume

```sql
CREATE OR REPLACE VIEW made
AS SELECT blender_id,
COUNT(*) as maded,
SUM(quantity) as sales_rate
FROM blendingtea, blendingtea_ordered
WHERE blendingtea.tea_id = blendingtea_ordered.tea_id GROUP BY blender_id;
```

**Explanation:**  
This view shows how many blending teas each blender belonging to the company has created, along with the total sales volume of those teas. Because `OR REPLACE` is explicitly used, re-executing the query allows the view to be updated whenever sales figures change or a new blending tea is developed.

***

### 5. View for a Customer to Check Purchase History

The following view allows a customer to check purchase history, including when the order was placed, which tea was ordered, how many units were ordered, and the total amount paid (`unit price * ordered quantity`).

The example below assumes that customer `C0001` checks purchase history.

```sql
CREATE OR REPLACE VIEW purchase_history
AS SELECT ordertable.order_id, order_date, blendingtea.tea_name, blendingtea_ordered.quantity, blendingtea_ordered.quantity * blendingtea.price as purchase_price
FROM ordertable,blendingtea_ordered,blendingtea
WHERE ordertable.order_id = blendingtea_ordered.order_id
AND blendingtea_ordered.tea_id = blendingtea.tea_id
AND ordertable.customer_id = 'C0001';
```

**Explanation:**  
Using `OR REPLACE VIEW` makes it possible to recreate the view whenever needed even if a view with the same name already exists. To determine which blending tea was ordered for a particular order (`ordertable.order_id`), the query joins `ordertable` with `blendingtea_ordered`. Since the unit price of the blending tea is also required, it additionally joins `blendingtea`, where price information is stored.

***

### 6. Automatically Inserting Subscription Orders into `ordertable` and `blendingtea_ordered`

For subscribers, new orders are inserted into `ordertable` and `blendingtea_ordered` on a regular basis according to the amount specified in `rate_plan` and the tea selected by the customer.

```sql
INSERT INTO ordertable (order_id,order_date,customer_id) select 'O0019', subscribe_date,customer_id from subscriber NATURAL JOIN rate_plan where customer_id='C0012' ;

INSERT INTO blendingtea_ordered (tea_id,order_id,quantity) select 'T0009', order_id,provided_amount from subscriber NATURAL JOIN rate_plan natural join ordertable where order_id='O0019';
```

**Explanation:**  
The `provided_amount` in the `rate_plan` table indicates how many teas are delivered monthly when a customer subscribes to that plan.

If customer `C0012`, who uses the subscription service, chooses to regularly receive the tea product with `Tea_ID = T0009`, then the quantity specified by the subscribed plan’s `provided_amount` should be delivered once per month. This is effectively equivalent to inserting an order into `ordertable` for that quantity, which is why the above SQL is needed.

Whenever the subscriber’s monthly subscription is updated, a new record is inserted into both `ordertable` and `blendingtea_ordered`. In the query above, when customer `C0012` is updated, a new order `O0019` is added after the previous order `O0018`, and a corresponding order record for `T0009` is also inserted into `blendingtea_ordered` according to the amount specified by the subscribed plan. In this example, since the customer is assumed to use the Basic Plan that provides 30 teas per month, an order for a quantity of 30 is generated.

### 7. Displaying Royalty by Blender Age Group (Average Age) in Ascending Order

```sql
SELECT royalty, AVG(blender.age) as Average_age 
FROM blender, blendingtea 
WHERE blender.blender_id = blendingtea.blender_id 
GROUP BY royalty 
ORDER BY AVG(blender.age);
```

**Explanation:**  
This query is used to observe the relationship between royalty level and blender age by grouping blenders according to royalty and showing the average age in ascending order.

***

### 8. Displaying Tea Names Developed by Blenders Under 30 by Age and Gender

```sql
SELECT age,gender, tea_name 
FROM blender, blendingtea 
WHERE blender.blender_id = blendingtea.blender_id and age<30 
GROUP BY age, gender, tea_name 
ORDER BY tea_name; 
```

**Explanation:**  
This query is used to understand what types of teas are developed depending on the age and gender of the blenders.

***

### 9. Displaying Subscription Plan Name, Price, and Tea Count in Descending Price Order

```sql
SELECT plan_name, price, provided_amount 
FROM rate_plan 
ORDER BY price DESC;
```

**Explanation:**  
This query shows the name of each subscription plan, its price, and the number of teas provided, sorted from the most expensive to the least expensive option.

***

### 10. Identifying Outstanding Employees by the Number of `A` Grades Received

To reward outstanding employees, the following query shows the names, phone numbers, and the number of `A` grades received by employees who took lectures, sorted by the number of `A` grades in descending order.

```sql
SELECT emp_name,phone_number, count(emp_id) as grade_A 
FROM lecture_taken NATURAL JOIN employee 
WHERE grade = 'A'
group by emp_id,emp_name,phone_number
order by grade_A DESC;
```

***

### 11. Identifying Subscribers Who Subscribed from December 2022 Onward

Since the company is preparing an event for recently subscribed customers, the following SQL retrieves the names, addresses (`Si`, `Dong`, `Ho`), login IDs, and subscription dates of customers who subscribed from **December 2022 onward**.

```sql
SELECT customer_name,si,dong,ho,login_id,subscribe_date
FROM customer NATURAL JOIN subscriber 
WHERE subscribe_date > '2022-11-30' 
ORDER BY subscribe_date DESC;
```

**Explanation:**  
By sorting in descending order of subscription date, it becomes easier to distinguish customers who subscribed or renewed more recently.

***

### 12. Counting Customers Born in the 1990s by Gender

```sql
SELECT gender,count(customer_id) as gender_count 
FROM customer 
WHERE birthdate > '1989-12-31' AND birthdate < '2000-01-01' 
GROUP BY gender;
```

**Explanation:**  
This query is used to determine how heavily the service is used by younger customers and which gender is dominant within that demographic.
